    Recap of the Health and Recovery from Major Injuries

    • QB
    • RB
    • WR
    • TE

    Kyler Murray

    ARI | 25 Years Old

    ACL + Meniscus

    Surgery Date: 1/3/23

    Return Timeline: October 2023

    Kyler underwent surgery in early January to reconstruct his ACL and repair his meniscus after he injured his right knee in Week 14 against New England. Murray will be limited all off-season and faces an uphill battle to be ready for Week 1. He's unlikely to return to 100% until late in the year given his January surgery. Historically, we tend to see a dip in rushing efficiency in the first year after ACL surgery in QBs, so fantasy managers should not expect his dynamic rushing ability to return until late November or December. He's a potential candidate to begin the year on PUP.

    Trey Lance

    SF | 23 Years Old

    Ankle Fracture + Dislocation

    Surgery Date: 9/18/22

    Return Timeline: OTAs

    Lance injured his right ankle in Week 2 against Seattle, suffering a season-ending injury. He underwent surgery shortly after the injury to stabilize the ankle, which typically requires a 6-9 month recovery. Look for Lance to ramp up his football activity throughout the offseason. I fully expect him to be available for OTAs, but he won't be 100% until training camp. Barring any setbacks, Lance's Week 1 availability is not in question, though it remains to be seen who wins the starting job for San Francisco in 2023.

    Jimmy Garoppolo

    LV | 31 Years Old

    Lisfranc Surgery

    Surgery Date: March 2023

    Return Timeline: Training Camp

    Garoppolo broke multiple bones in his left foot on December 6, 2022 when he was playing for the 49ers. At the time of the injury, the team believed that he would be able to avoid surgery and rehab the injury. He of course was unable to return during the playoffs and signed a deal with Las Vegas in free agency. News broke at OTAs in late May that Garoppolo would be unavailable to participate in the team's off-season program due to the fact that he's recovering from surgery on his previously injured foot. It was reported that the Raiders learned Jimmy G would need surgery during his physical with his new team earlier this off-season. It's likely his Lisfranc injury didn't completely heal after the initial injury. As a result, the Raiders QB will be rehabbing his foot for most of the summer, though based on the timeline, he will likely be cleared at some point during training camp. At this time, he's not in danger of missing regular season games, but he'll likely miss valuable time to gel with his new team this off-season.

    Ryan Tannehill

    TEN | 34 Years Old

    High Ankle Sprain

    Surgery Date: Dec. 2022

    Return Timeline: OTAs

    The Titans QB suffered multiple high ankle sprains in 2022, but it was his right ankle injury in Week 15 that forced him to have tightrope surgery on his repeatedly injured right ankle, ending his season. This recovery usually takes about 6 weeks, so this will not affect Tanehill's 2023 off-season or regular season. He'll be ready to go for OTAs.

    Tua Tagovailoa

    MIA | 25 Years Old

    Concussions

    Return Timeline: Ready for Offseason Program

    Tua's 2022 season was full of ups and downs but was largely defined by suffering three concussions in the same season as well as two in the same week. Obviously, this situation is concerning for Tua's long term health and dynasty outlook. A 2019 literature review found that athletes with a concussion history are 3.76 times more likely to have an additional concussion, and they're more likely to suffer a lower body injury as well. The reason for this is because of the brain's role in balance and stability. In addition, other studies have shown that athletes who suffer subsequent concussions are more likely to take longer to recover from their concussions.

    Lamar Jackson

    BAL | 26 Years Old

    PCL Sprain

    Return Timeline: OTAs

    Lamar Jackson sprained the PCL in his left knee in Week 13 against the Broncos, suffering a grade 2 injury. He did not play the remainder of the season and now enters the offseason with question marks about the status of his knee. Fortunately, these injuries do heal over time, but when an athlete attempts to recover from a grade 2 sprain, it can take longer than the typical 4-6 week recovery timeline that's widely reported. Some athletes deal with lingering instability, discomfort and swelling in the knee for weeks, if not months. However, athletes are able to return to form following this injury with enough time and the proper rehab plan. Jackson will get stronger as the offseason progresses and be back to 100% by the time training camp comes around in August. He will be able to remain a dynamic rushing QB that makes him so lethal in fantasy.

    Brock Purdy

    SF | 23 Years Old

    Torn UCL

    Surgery Date: 3/10/23

    Return Timeline: Training Camp

    Brock Purdy suffered a torn ulnar collateral ligament (UCL) in his right elbow against the Eagles in the NFC Championship. This ligament is known as the Tommy John ligament in baseball pitchers, and without this ligament intact, throwing athletes cannot effectively do their job. As a result, Purdy underwent surgery in early March to repair the ligament with an Internal Brace. This procedure is favorable for Purdy's short term outlook as the repair with an internal brace usually requires about a six month timeline, giving him an outside shot at being ready for Week 1. However, because the athlete must take time to ramp up his throwing volume and velocity, Purdy is unlikely to be involved in the Niners' offseason program. He should get fully cleared at some point during training camp, but he'll start throwing in a limited capacity at some point in in early June. With this injury, the Niners signed Sam Darnold to a one-year deal, and he'll likely split the vast majority of reps throughout the offseason with Trey Lance, who is recovering from ankle surgery.

    Matthew Stafford

    LAR | 35 Years Old

    Spinal Cord Contusion

    Return Timeline: Ready for Offseason Program

    Stafford's season came to a close in Week 11 due to a serious neck injury that resulted in a spinal cord contusion. This injury is one that cannot be taken lightly as it has not only short term but also long term implications if not handled appropriately. Fortunately, it sounds like Stafford is recovering as expected and confirmed in late December that he will not be retiring and is expecting to return for a 15th season. For now, this is an ongoing situation, but I expect Stafford to be back to football activity this summer, if not before.

    Breece Hall

    NYJ | 22 Years Old

    ACL + Meniscus

    Surgery Date: 11/18/22

    Return Timeline: Limited throughout the offseason, has a chance to be ready Week 1

    The Jets RB tore the ACL and meniscus in his left knee in Week 7 and underwent surgery on November 18, roughly one month after the date of his injury. While this may seem confusing to most, this is actually good news when it comes to Hall's long term health as delaying surgery and rehabbing for roughly a month prior to ACL surgery improves outcomes following surgery. In addition, research shows that NFL RBs who are successful after ACL surgery tend to be productive prior to injury, be younger and have top-2 round draft capital. Hall checks all the boxes that suggests he’s likely to return to form. The issue, however, is that because his surgery was later in the season, it's likely we don't see the best version of Hall until later in the season. Look for Hall to really regain his form in 2024 and get off to a slower start in 2023. As of late May, Hall was not participating in OTAs.

    Isiah Pacheco

    KC | 24 Years Old

    Hand and Shoulder Surgeries

    Surgery Date: Late February

    Return Timeline: Training Camp

    News broke in May that Pacheco is currently recovering from two surgeries following their Super Bowl run in 2022. The Chiefs RB broke a bone in his thumb in the AFC Championship game but played through the injury against the Eagles and ultimately had surgery one week after the season ended. He also played through a torn labrum in his shoulder for "much of the year" and had surgery to repair the labrum two weeks after the Super Bowl. This recovery timeline typically takes 5-6 months but can take longer for athletes in contact sports to get back on the field. At this point in the off-season, Pacheco's six month timeline would put him back on the field right around mid to late August, adding some mild uncertainty about his Week 1 availability. Expect Pacheco to sport a non-contact jersey for most of the Chiefs' off-season program as his shoulder heals. The broken bone in his thumb will not have an impact on his 2023 outlook as that will be healed well in advance of training camp.

    Javonte Williams

    DEN | 23 Years Old

    ACL + LCL + Posterolateral Corner

    Surgery Date: Week of October 10, 2022

    Return Timeline: Limited throughout the offseason, likely to start camp on PUP

    Williams suffered his season-ending injury in Week 4, tearing his ACL and LCL in his right knee. In addition, Williams' injury also included damage to what's called the "Posterolateral corner" which refers to a complex group of ligaments and tendons at the back of the knee. This injury is significant as it takes more than a year for athletes to recover from this type of multi-ligament injury before that player is back to 100%. Compared to an isolated ACL injury, this rehab is much slower and takes a lot longer to reach peak performance. It would not be surprising at all to see Williams miss the vast majority of Denver's offseason program and start camp on the PUP list. There's a chance Williams misses multiple games to start the 2023 season, and from a fantasy perspective, his best football likely won't be played until 2024. Denver signed Samaje Perine to a two-year deal in free agency to help add depth while Williams recovers from this injury.

    Jonathan Taylor

    IND | 24 Years Old

    High Ankle Sprain

    Surgery Date: 1/25/23

    Return Timeline: OTAs

    Taylor missed most of the 2022 season due to multiple ankle injuries. It was ultimately a right high ankle sprain in Week 15 that ended his season, which is notable because it was the same ankle that Taylor injured earlier in the season. These repeated ankle injuries on the same side can lead to laxity in the ligaments and discomfort in the joint, creating an increased risk of re-injury in the future. As a result, JT underwent a surgical procedure on his ankle in late January. The procedure was described as an arthroscopic debridement, which is essentially a "clean out" procedure to remove scar tissue and debris in the joint. This type of recovery is relatively straight forward for athletes, so there isn't a cumbersome rehab associated with this surgery. Taylor may be limited early in the offseason, but there's no doubt he'll be back to 100% by the time camp opens up this summer.

    Dameon Pierce

    HOU | 23 Years Old

    High Ankle Sprain

    Return Timeline: OTAs

    Dameon Pierce's rookie season came to an end in Week 14 when he picked up a high ankle sprain against Dallas. At the time of injury, Pierce's sprain was described as "mild" but given that the Texans had no chance of making the postseason, he was shut down for the rest of the year and placed on IR. When asked about being placed on IR, here's what Pierce had to say: "It was an administrative decision to put me on IR so I could get into my offseason program heading into next year. Just making sure I was ready and healthy enough to get a jumpstart on my offseason program." Entering year two, Pierce won't have any limitations during the offseason program and will enter the regular season at 100%.

    Tony Pollard

    DAL | 26 Years Old

    Fractured Fibula + High Ankle Sprain

    Surgery Date: 1/24/23

    Return Timeline: Limited throughout the offseason, has a chance to be ready for Week 1

    Pollard fractured his fibula and suffered a high ankle sprain on his left leg in the Divisional Round of the Playoffs. This recovery usually takes about 6 months before the athlete is truly back to 100%, but it can take longer depending the degree of associated ligament damage. Pollard was set to hit free agency, but he's back in Dallas on the Franchise Tag. While it's not unreasonable to expect a dip in Pollard's production in the early part of 2023, this injury shouldn't have a major impact on his long-term outlook. Dallas also designated Zeke as a post June 1 cut, potentially signaling that they feel confident in Pollard's 2023 outlook as their lead back.

    Rashaad Penny

    PHI | 27 Years Old

    Fractured Fibula + High Ankle Sprain

    Surgery Date: 10/11/22

    Return Timeline: Training Camp

    Penny suffered a season-ending ankle injury in Week 5 to stabilize a fractured fibula, syndesmotic ligament tear and a torn deltoid ligament. This injury is significant and requires about a 6 month rehab process. Fortunately for Penny, this injury happened early enough in the season that he's got a great chance to be ready for Week 1 of the 2023 season. The bad news, however, is that Penny is 27 years old, has a significant history of lower body injuries. It'll be tough for Penny to carve out a work horse role in the NFL given his injuries. Penny signed a cheap one-year deal with Philadelphia during free agency, which seems to be a good fit given the workload concerns due to his past injuries. Expect the Eagles to use multiple backs this season with Penny the favorite for early down work.

    Tyjae Spears

    TEN | 22 Years Old

    ACL Injuries

    Surgery Date: 2020

    Return Timeline: N/A

    Tyjae Spears, the rookie RB out of Tulane, was selected 81st overall by the Tennessee Titans in the 2023 NFL Draft. He's a player who's flying up rookie rankings in dynasty leagues as a result. Spears enters the NFL with health concerns due to a lengthy history of knee injuries. He tore his ACL in high school then re-tore the same ACL in 2020 while in college. Despite a productive final season at Tulane, Spears reportedly has full-thickness cartilage loss and arthritis in his knee. There's also conflicting reports that Spears may not have an intact ACL in his knee. As we've seen with other RBs (Todd Gurley, Jay Ajayi for example) who enter the NFL with degenerative changes in the knee, there is a concern that his career could be shortened. In dynasty formats it's something to consider as it's possible Spears only plays a handful of seasons in the NFL depending on how his knee responds.

    Antonio Gibson

    WAS | 25 Years Old

    Foot Fracture

    Surgery Date: 1/5/23

    Return Timeline: Situation to monitor; Ready for Training Camp

    Gibson was limited during the back half of the season with a left foot injury as well as a knee sprain. He ultimately had his season cut short after requiring surgery on his foot for what the team called a fracture. It's difficult to comment on the specifics of this injury without knowing what part of the foot was involved. At the end of the season, the coaching staff expressed optimism that Gibson will be ready for OTAs. For now, this is a situation to monitor.

    Cooper Kupp

    LAR | 30 Years Old

    High Ankle Sprain

    Surgery Date: 11/16/22

    Return Timeline: OTAs

    Kupp's 2022 season came to an end in Week 10 when he suffered a significant right high ankle sprain against the Cardinals. He underwent tightrope surgery to stabilize the sprain and has been rehabbing since mid November. There's no doubt that Kupp will be ready for the start of training camp and is even likely to be cleared for OTAs. He'll get stronger and more explosive off that right ankle as the summer progresses and will enter 2023 without any limitations.

    Allen Robinson II

    PIT | 29 Years Old

    Stress Fracture

    Surgery Date: Week of 11/28/22

    Return Timeline: Training Camp

    Robinson's 2022 season was disappointing for many reasons. After failing to produce consistently with the Rams, his season was cut short due to a stress fracture in the navicular bone of his left foot. This bone is located on the inside of the arch of the foot and is stressed during the running and cutting motion, making it an injury that's very difficult to play through. Robinson will be ready well ahead of Week 1 of the 2023 season but is likely to be limited early in the offseason. As of early January, Robinson was still not weight bearing on his left foot, making it unlikely he's 100% until later this summer.

    Rashod Bateman

    BAL | 23 Years Old

    Lisfranc Surgery

    Surgery Date: 11/18/22

    Return Timeline: Training Camp

    Bateman only played six games in 2022 due to a foot sprain that ultimately resulted in a season-ending Lisfranc injury that took place in mid-November. He had a screw inserted into the foot to stabilize the injury, suggesting there was some instability involved. Recovery following this procedure is usually predictable, but Bateman missed time in mini camp after receiving a cortisone injection. The specific reason for this is unclear, but it's possible this suggest Bateman was dealing with pain and/or irritation when trying to ramp up his workload this spring. In my experience, a cortisone injection is not used as part of a rehab plan. Rather, it's used as an anti-inflammatory for pain control. The recovery for this surgery usually takes somewhere between 6-9 months, but this cortisone injection does signal that Bateman might take longer than usual to get back to 100%. For now, this is a situation to monitor ahead of training camp. Look for his explosiveness and speed to improve as the season goes on.

    DJ Chark Jr.

    CAR | 26 Years Old

    Ankle Surgery

    Surgery Date: March 2023

    Return Timeline: Training Camp

    Chark missed six games last year in Detroit due to a high ankle sprain. In late March, new head coach Frank Reich told reporters that Chark underwent surgery on his ankle and said the hope is this procedure will keep Chark from continuing to have some of the foot/ankle injuries he’s experienced over the last couple of seasons. This sounds like a cleanup procedure, which shouldn't affect Chark's Week 1 availability. He was seen participating in a limited capacity at OTAs, so he's got a great to chance to be a full participant early in training camp.

    Michael Thomas

    NO | 30 Years Old

    Dislocated Toe

    Surgery Date: Early November 2022

    Return Timeline: Limited throughout the offseason, likely to be ready for Week 1

    Thomas' foot and ankle injuries are well documented at this point after he missed nearly all of 2020 and 2021 while recovering from ankle surgery. Unfortunately for Thomas, it was more of the same in 2022 as his season was cut short due to a dislocated toe in Week 3. He underwent surgery on his second toe on his right foot in early November. Note, it was the left ankle that required surgery in 2021. Thomas has now played just four games from 2020 - 2022 due to foot, ankle and toe injuries. Because of these injuries, it'll be an uphill battle for Thomas to return to his pre-injury level of play from 2016-2019. Thomas turns 30 in early March and is back in New Orleans on a one-year deal.

    Darnell Mooney

    CHI | 25 Years Old

    Fractured Fibula + High Ankle Sprain

    Surgery Date: Early December 2022

    Return Timeline: Limited throughout the offseason, will be ready by Week 1

    Mooney suffered a significant left high ankle sprain in Week 12 against the Jets that required surgery to stabilize the torn ligaments. The specifics on Mooney's surgery are not known at this time. However, given the timeline of the injury, Mooney will be ready for Week 1 of the 2023 season. He is likely to be limited throughout the offseason, however.

    Wan'Dale Robinson

    NYG | 22 Years Old

    ACL

    Surgery Date: 12/13/22

    Return Timeline: Limited throughout the offseason, chance to be ready by Week 1

    Robinson tore the ACL in his right knee in Week 11 and underwent surgery roughly a month later in mid December. By all accounts, this injury was an isolated tear, meaning there is not additional damage to the meniscus or other knee ligaments. This bodes well for Robinson's outlook, but that doesn't mean he'll be 100% by the start of the regular season. He's got a chance to be ready for Week 1, but given the timing of his surgery, it's likely Robinson's best football is played in November and December vs. early in the year.

    Sterling Shepard

    NYG | 30 Years Old

    ACL

    Surgery Date: 10/25/22

    Return Timeline: Limited throughout the offseason, chance to be ready by Week 1

    The Giants veteran WR tore the ACL in his left knee in Week 3 and underwent surgery in late October. Shepard is likely to be ready close to the start of the regular season, but like most players coming off ACL surgery, he'll need time before he's truly at 100%. At 30 years old with an ACL and Achilles injury in his profile, he's unlikely to return to his prior level of play, however. He's back in New York on a one-year deal, but he'll likely be a role player as he works his way back from this injury.

    Michael Gallup

    DAL | 27 Years Old

    Knee and Ankle Scope

    Surgery Date: 1/31/23

    Return Timeline: Limited early in the offseason, 100% by training camp

    Gallup had two surgeries done on his right leg in late January - an arthroscopic procedure on his knee and ankle. While the details of the ankle injury are unknown, the knee procedure was done to address a meniscus injury. Gallup has now had several surgeries since he entered the NFL - a knee scope for his left meniscus in 2019, an ACL reconstruction in his right knee in early 2022 and now a procedure on both his right knee and ankle. Based on the description of his most recent surgeries, it doesn't sound like these involved any major repairs, so it's very likely that Gallup is 100% before training camp, though he will be limited early in the offseason. From a dynasty perspective, Gallup's injury history is starting to pile up when you consider he's had three total surgeries done on his knees in just a few years, but for 2023, there's a reason to be optimistic about Gallup's health. Last season, he struggled in year one off the ACL surgery, but he should be more explosive this season now that's he's back to 100%.

    Tim Patrick

    DEN | 29 Years Old

    ACL

    Surgery Date: 8/8/22

    Return Timeline: Limited throughout the offseason, ready for Training Camp

    Patrick tore his ACL in Broncos training camp in early August and underwent surgery shortly after. Patrick, of course, missed the entire season, but the good news and silver lining for Patrick is that he'll be ready well ahead of Week 1 for 2023 given the timing of his surgery. Look for Patrick's football activity to be ramped up over the course of the next several months. He'll ultimately get cleared for the start of training camp and be good to go for Week 1.

    Kyle Pitts

    ATL | 22 Years Old

    MCL

    Surgery Date: 11/29/22

    Return Timeline: Training Camp

    Pitts' 2022 season came to an end in Week 11 when he took a direct hit to the right knee against Chicago, resulting in a Grade 2 MCL injury. Fortunately, this recovery does not take nearly as long as a surgery for a torn ACL, so Pitts will be 100% well before the start of the regular season. He may be limited early in the off-season but by the time training camp starts, Pitts will be ready to go.

    Hayden Hurst

    CAR | 29 Years Old

    Hernia Repair Surgery

    Surgery Date: Early 2023

    Return Timeline: Training Camp

    Hurst signed a 3-year deal with Carolina in free agency, but he wasn't available to participate at OTAs in May as he's recovering from surgery to address a sports hernia. Last year in Cincinnati, Hurst missed a few games due to a calf injury, but he didn't miss any time due to any core or groin injuries, suggesting he was likely playing through the injury in 2022. Even though he didn't participate fully in OTAs, this injury shouldn't affect his effectiveness in 2023. Look for Hurst to be 100% in training camp.

    Zach Ertz

    ARI | 32 Years Old

    ACL + MCL

    Surgery Date: Week of Nov. 14, 2022

    Return Timeline: Limited throughout the offseason, chance to be ready by Week 1

    Ertz tore the ACL and MCL in his left knee in Week 10 and underwent surgery to repair these ligaments in mid November. Assuming his recovery goes well, is likely that Ertz will be ready to go in Week 1. That said, it's likely Ertz is not 100% until later in the season. He turns 33 in November.
