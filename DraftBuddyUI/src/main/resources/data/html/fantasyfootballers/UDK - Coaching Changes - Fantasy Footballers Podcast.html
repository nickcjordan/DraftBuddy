    Fantasy Implications for Coaching Moves This Offseason

    Head Coach

    Matt Eberflus, CHI

    Eberflus’ legacy will be intertwined with the development of Justin Fields. The team traded the house for him moving up last year leaving them with no first-round picks in 2022. The offensive line needs major help after Fields registered one of the worst sack rates (11.8) of all time. The upside is still there with his dual-threat ability to land near the top-12 among fantasy QBs at the end of the year. However, the downside is this might end up being one of the worst teams in the NFL. It's not a great look hiring a defensive-minded coach on a team with a lack of offensive talent. That recipe usually ends up leading to a team tanking and rebuilding for the next 2-3 years.

    Head Coach

    Nathaniel Hackett, DEN

    After starting the year 3-0 due to a soft schedule, the Broncos ended at 7-10 and Vic Fangio was out the door. Hackett had a ton of success in Green Bay but he also was tied to a Hall of Fame QB in Aaron Rodgers. Well, a couple of months on the job, and Hackett was gifted with another HOFer in Russell Wilson. Hopefully, he will be able to let Russ do his thing in the kitchen and the offense can be more free-flowing to suit his strengths. Denver has morphed from the early teenage pubescent years of Drew Lock to a legit Super Bowl contender with a defense that is a top-10 unit as well.

    Head Coach

    Lovie Smith, HOU

    You have to feel slightly bad for David Culley who really was in a no-win situation with the Texans lasting one year. With by far the worst roster in the NFL, finishing at 4-12 isn’t too bad. Lovie Smith is brought in despite not being a head coach in the NFL since 2015, back when Avengers Age of Ultron was a new hit movie. With the lowest Vegas win total (4) in the league, it should be a rough year with the Texans.

    Head Coach

    Doug Pederson, JAX

    Flush it down the toilet. All of it. All of the Urban Meyer filth you remember from 2021 and maybe give Trevor Lawrence a second chance? It was an abysmal year across the board as Jacksonville averaged a league-low 14.9 points per game and hit their team implied total in just four of their 17 games. Pederson hopefully can use some of that magic he had in Philadelphia to help redirect the franchise. The AFC South is arguably the weakest division in the NFL so there is room for Jacksonville to improve dramatically from three wins.

    Head Coach

    Josh McDaniels, LV

    It’s wild to think it’s been 12 years since McDaniels was the head coach of the Broncos. [Editor’s Note: It did not go well.] The Raiders’ offense was better than you might realize in 2021 moving the ball effectively en route to a WildCard playoff spot. With the extension of Derek Carr and trading for best buddy Davante Adams, it's clear where the Raiders have placed their chips. However, the competition in the AFC West is absolutely brutal and the defense still needs an overhaul in the secondary. The verdict is still out on whether McDaniels can be a head coach in the NFL.

    Head Coach

    Mike McDaniel, MIA

    The young, budding offensive mind gets to design plays with possibly the most electric 1-2 WR combo in the league. Tyreek Hill and Jaylen Waddle will drive defenses bonkers and McDaniel preached how he wants to get Jaylen Waddle uber-involved in the game plan the same way they used Deebo Samuel in San Francisco. In theory, the Kyle Shanahan-style offense makes sense but the personnel is much different in Miami. They ranked 32nd in PFF’s end-of-season offensive line rankings allowing the most pressures in the NFL. Tua Tagovailoa‘s maturation is the biggest step for this offense while figuring out who their RB will be for fantasy is a complete conundrum.

    Head Coach

    Kevin O'Connell, MIN

    O’Connell is carrying the musk of Sean McVay and a successful Super Bowl run in their back pockets. The situation in Minnesota is ripe and we should expect business as usual with the offensive weapons. Justin Jefferson is elite. Dalvin Cook is awesome. If Kirk Cousins is officially back, they should continue trucking and could compete in the NFC North with the Packers.

    Head Coach

    Dennis Allen, NO

    The Sean Payton era is over but things shouldn’t be that different in New Orleans. Allen has been the long-time defensive coordinator and steadily improved his unit. New Orleans’ defense was stout ranking 4th in points allowed and the fewest yards per carry allowed (3.7) on the ground. On the offensive side of the ball, Pete Carmichael Jr. has been in his role for 14 years! The Saints were on the doorstep of the playoffs last year and with a hopefully healthy Michael Thomas along with adding Chris Olave, their playmakers are set up to restore the offense to relevance.

    Head Coach

    Brian Daboll, NYG

    This organization was an absolute train wreck as Joe Judge (Mr. Testosterone) and GM Dave Gettleman (IT’S ME DAVE!) were shown the door. Daboll will have his work cut out for him and while Daniel Jones does share some comparable traits with Josh Allen, it’s hard envisioning the high-end production for fantasy coming in his 4th year. Their cap situation isn’t great, the roster needs a big makeover, and Saquon Barkley also is in a contract year. Overall, the hire is good for fantasy but does it translate to success on the field?

    Offensive Coordinator

    Ken Dorsey, BUF

    The former University of Miami QB (remember that 2001 team?) steps in for Brian Daboll. Expect much of the same here in Buffalo with Josh Allen at the helm.

    Offensive Coordinator

    Ben McAdoo, CAR

    You won’t find many McAdoo truthers out there. He's possibly one of the most unflattering human beings on the eyeballs. Not a looker. Consider him a Humpty Dumpty with a mustache. This is one of the more curious hires of the off-season as he hasn’t had any role-calling plays since 2017. A lot has changed in the NFL since then Ben. In case you needed a push in the right direction, Sam Darnold ain't it.

    Offensive Coordinator

    Ben Johnson, DET

    Johnson saw an increased role into passing game coordinator in the 2nd half of season. Anthony Lynn, on the other hand, did not work out so well as a retread coach for Guns Mahoney.

    Offensive Coordinator

    Adam Stenavich, GB

    Rodgers and LaFleur are the real offensive coordinators here. It is worth noting that he expressed interest early this off-season in getting both RBs Aaron Jones and AJ Dillon in packages where they're on the field at the same time.

    Offensive Coordinator

    Pep Hamilton, HOU

    Lovie Smith pounded the table for Pep Hamilton to be the guy to direct the offense after he was the QB coach last year. Davis Mills surprised everyone but the offense still sputtered finishing dead last in total yards and a non-existent rushing game. Props to Pep for wanting to run it back but this team is going to have a hard time sustaining drives.

    Offensive Coordinator

    Liam Coen, LAR

    McVay runs this show… We get it. But here's a fun fact: Coen was the QB at UMass where he was roommates with former Giants star WR Victor Cruz. Break that little piece of trivia out next time you're with your friends. You can thank us later.
