    Fantasy Implications for the Biggest Offseason Moves

    • QB
    • RB
    • WR
    • TE
    • Free Agents
    signed

    Lamar Jackson

    Signed | 26 Years Old

    Ravens, 5 years

    Entering the off-season, Jackson received the Non-Exclusive Tag from the Ravens, raising questions about his future. Those questions have been answered after he agreed to a monster 5-year deal worth $260 million ($185M guaranteed). Jackson has missed ten games over the last two years while seeing his TD rate plummet and his INT rate rise, but he's still the greatest rushing QB off all time and at 26 years old, he still has a lot of years left to perform for fantasy.

    signed

    Derek Carr

    Signed | 32 Years Old

    Saints, 4 years

    After nine years in the silver and black, Carr will need to add gold to his wardrobe. Carr should provide some stability to a Saints quarterback room that never quite settled after Drew Brees' retirement. Keep in mind that Carr never has finished in the top-12 of fantasy QBs in his career so don't expect any sort of ceiling.

    signed

    Daniel Jones

    Signed | 26 Years Old

    Giants, 4 Years

    Maybe you didnt look up and realize Daniel Jones finished as the QB9 in points per game this season. Yes, that Daniel Jones! He led the league with a 1.1% INT rate and an 81.1% adjusted completion rate (subtracts drops) but finished with only 15 passing TDs. His fantasy production can be traced back to what he accomplished on the ground becoming one of 12 QBs since 1973 to rush for 700 yards and seven TDs in a season. He receives a 4-year deal at roughly $35 million per year.

    signed

    Geno Smith

    Signed | 32 Years Old

    Seahawks, 3 Years

    It was probably a blur for you but Geno Smith ended up with the 4th most passing TDs in the league and the QB5 on the year! Imagine telling someone that before the season From Week 3 on, he had ten top-12 finishes, which was as many as Joe Burrow. He led all QBs with 14 TDs of 20+ yards accounting for an astounding 47 percent of his passing TDs! The veteran played his way into a three year extension worth up to $105 million.

    signed

    Jimmy Garoppolo

    Signed | 31 Years Old

    Raiders, 3 Years

    To quote Michael Scott, "Snip snap snip snap!" Jimmy G went from about to be traded to "oh no!" the 49ers need him to franchise savior to injured yet again. He's a solid bridge QB option and landed as a starter with Las Vegas on a 3-year deal with $34 million guaranteed. Don't be surprised if they take a QB at 7th overall to slowly develop behind Jimmy Handsome.

    signed

    Baker Mayfield

    Signed | 28 Years Old

    Buccaneers, 1 year

    Baker's career has been full of ups-and-downs. From #1 overall pick and stellar rookie year, to the Freddie Kitchens coaching nightmare, years of insurance commercials and underachieving, to getting trading to the Rams after Deshaun Watson was acquired. Now, he lands in Tampa on a one-year deal getting paid backup money ($8.5 million). As of now, it looks like he'll compete with Kyle Trask to be the starter.

    Carson Wentz

    Unsigned | 30 Years Old

    Years from now, scholars will study the roller coaster ride of Carson James Wentz and ask themselves: where did it all go wrong? The No. 2 overall pick in 2017 was an MVP candidate, run out of Philadelphia, Indianapolis, and then essentially benched in Washington. He hasn't been the best locker room guy and the Commanders responded by cutting him for exactly zero dollars. In other words, so long Wentz.

    signed

    Jacoby Brissett

    Signed | 30 Years Old

    Commanders

    Brissett was actually quite competent last year with the Browns. In the 11 games he started before Deshaun Watson took over, the Browns ran the 2nd most plays in the NFL per game and were top-12 in points scored. He's a solid backup when called upon and it looks like he could have an opportunity to compete for the starting job if Sam Howell falters.

    signed

    Marcus Mariota

    Signed | 29 Years Old

    Eagles, 1 year

    Mariota started the first 12 games before being injured, getting demoted, and eventually going on IR for the rest of the year. Atlanta saved roughly $12 million by cutting the former No. 2 overall pick. Desmond Ridder started the final five games and this franchise will likely roll with Ridder or if they take a shot at a rookie in the 1st. As for Mariota, he's off to Philly to backup Jalen Hurts on a one-year deal.

    signed

    Jameis Winston

    Signed | 29 Years Old

    Saints, 1 Year

    Yes, Jameis is still here in New Orleans. It felt like the team forgot that as he was essentially benched for Andy Dalton despite returning from injury. With Derek Carr inked as the starter, Winston was brought back to hold the clipboard yet again. He's fallen a long way from being the former No. 1 overall pick.

    signed

    Taylor Heinicke

    Signed | 30 Years Old

    Falcons, 2 Years

    If you're on team #TryHard, Heinicke is the poster boy. He's someone you root for but it's a train wreck waiting to happen on the football field. He signed on with the Falcons to backup Desmond Ridder.

    signed

    Sam Darnold

    Signed | 26 Years Old

    49ers, 1 Year

    It's been an utter disaster for the former No. 3 overall pick. To be fair, he was respectable going 4-2 in the final six games of the season but with a career 60 percent completion rate, Darnold is doomed for being labeled a massive bust and "what could've been". He signed on with San Francisco to compete for a backup job.

    signed

    Mike White

    Signed | 28 Years Old

    Dolphins, 2 Years

    There are some Mike White truthers out there who thought he was ready to be the Jets franchise QB. The offense definitely was more exciting as the Jets averaged 72 plays per game (#1 in the NFL) in his starts the last two years. The team is also 2-5 in those starts. He lands in division with the Dolphins as a competent backup to Tua Tagovailoa.

    signed

    Gardner Minshew

    Signed | 27 Years Old

    Colts, 1 Year

    The gift that keeps on giving: Gardner Minshew. He signed on with the Colts for a one year deal after performing admirably as the Eagles backup in two starts last year.

    signed

    Andy Dalton

    Signed | 35 Years Old

    Panthers, 2 years

    It's wild to think a 35-year old Andy Dalton somehow started 14 games for a Saints team that was on the brink of playoff contention until the final week of the season. He actually was respectable posting his highest yards per attempt and QBR since 2015. Carolina brought him in on a cheap 2-year deal to be the mentor for whichever rookie QB they take with the #1 overall pick in April's draft.

    signed

    Case Keenum

    Signed | 35 Years Old

    Texans, 2 Years

    Keenum backed up Josh Allen last year and now has the opportunity to provide some veteran leadership to the Texans' franchise QB they select at No. 2 overall.

    Teddy Bridgewater

    Unsigned | 30 Years Old

    "Teddy Two Gloves" is one of those players you root for, you're glad he's on an NFL roster, but you can safely disregard any notion that he will have fantasy relevance. He could run it back with Miami as Tua Tagovailoa's backup.

    Tyler Huntley

    Unsigned | 25 Years Old

    Everyone's favorite Pro Bowl games QB and Ravens playoff starter has been a serviceable backup for Lamar Jackson but the offensive system in Baltimore is changing with OC Greg Roman out the door. He's only 25 years old so he likely will find a home somewhere holding a clipboard.

    • Free Agents
    • Cut Candidates

    Dalvin Cook

    Unsigned | 27 Years Old

    The Vikings saved themselves almost $8 million by moving on from Dalvin Cook in early June. Entering his age 28 season, Cook is coming off four straight seasons of 1,100+ rushing yards but has struggled with recurring shoulder injuries. Coming off shoulder surgery to address those issues, we expect him to sign a short term deal with a contending team. Alexander Mattison is now the RB1 in Minnesota.

    franchised

    Saquon Barkley

    Franchised | 26 Years Old

    Giants

    Saquon picked a great time to have a career resurgence. Heading into a contract year, the 5th year RB finished as the RB6 thanks to a monster workload with the 4th most carries, 6th most RB targets, and the 3rd most expected fantasy points. He just turned 26 years old and was welcomed back to New York with open arms on a one-year franchise tag.

    franchised

    Josh Jacobs

    Franchised | 25 Years Old

    Raiders

    Jacobs morphed from solid fantasy back into a dominant workhorse in 2022 with the 5th most rushing yards (1,653) in a season over the last decade. He led the league in forced missed tackles and was the RB1 from Week 9 on. Jacobs just turned 25 years old and the Raiders happily franchised the star RB.

    franchised

    Tony Pollard

    Franchised | 26 Years Old

    Cowboys

    The 4th year RB was a revelation for those who were able to navigate early in the season. He averaged 0.99 fantasy points per touch giving you a simple math equation every week: how many touches is he going to get? He ended up with the 6th most RB TDs, 2nd in Yards per Reception, and the 3rd most 15+ yard runs. Pollard, unfortunately, was carted off the field against the 49ers in the playoffs with a fractured fibula. Assuming his recovery goes according to plan, Pollard has a good shot at being ready for Week 1, but it would not be surprising to see him get off to a slower start. He was franchised by Dallas for $10.09 million.

    signed

    Miles Sanders

    Signed | 26 Years Old

    Panthers, 4 years

    Sanders finally broke through with a complete season for fantasy thanks to the 4th most red zone touches among RBs and a Philadelphia offense that took a major step forward in 2022. Carolina inked the former 2019 2nd round pick to a 4-year deal worth up to $25 million, which tells us he should be in line for a hefty workload as the RB1. Carolina returns all five starting offensive linemen, and last year, this unit ranked #9 in Football Outsiders' adjusted line yards metric. Of course, this offense is likely to be led by a rookie QB so there's some systemic concerns on the offense as a whole, but the workload should be there for Sanders.

    signed

    David Montgomery

    Signed | 26 Years Old

    Lions, 3 years

    It feels like Montgomery has been a Chicago Bear for a while but he picked the wrong year to enter free agency. He's proven he can be a reliable option for an NFL franchise, averaging 289 opportunities per year since he entered the NFL. Monty stays in the NFC North, landing on a Lions depth chart that also includes D'Andre Swift. The signing is a bit concerning for how Detroit views Swift, but as we saw with Jamaal Williams, there's a path for multiple RBs to be fantasy viable. We expect Montgomery to inherit the coveted goal line role for Dan Campbell's team.

    Ezekiel Elliott

    Unsigned | 27 Years Old

    The air finally came out of the tires and Zeke is officially out on the street looking for work. His body of work is impressive starting his career with six straight RB1 seasons and averaging 1231 rushing yards and 9.3 TDs per year. But at this point, it's hard imagining him being anything more than a committee back with a "big name".

    Leonard Fournette

    Unsigned | 28 Years Old

    Lenny's days in Tampa are over after the team cut him, which is not surprising as the team looks towards the future in the middle of a rebuild. Rachaad White should be heavily involved in 2023 as the de facto starter with Ke'Shawn Vaughn there as a backup. Fournette, 28, is likely to land a short term deal as a backup, but this free agency class is loaded, and this Draft class is strong. Don't expect any NFL franchises to break the bank for the Dump Truck.

    Kareem Hunt

    Unsigned | 27 Years Old

    Entering a contract year, you'd think the Browns would milk Hunt of everything he's worth or at least package him at the trade deadline. They did neither as Hunt was curiously irrelevant for fantasy with just two games with double-digit fantasy points all year long. He's still only 27 years old with the skillset to sign on as a committee back and see 10-12 touches per game.

    signed

    Devin Singletary

    Signed | 25 Years Old

    Texans, 1 year

    It's possible we've seen the best years of Singletary's career. The Buffalo offense was loaded but apart from an awesome end of season run in 2021, he's always had to compete for touches among other RBs (Zack Moss & James Cook) and the Bills' true goal-line back: Josh Allen. Now he joins the Texans backfield. With a new coaching regime, it's unknown how they will split opportunities between Dameon Pierce and Devin Singletary.

    signed

    Jamaal Williams

    Signed | 28 Years Old

    Saints, 3 years

    The hips didn't lie as Jamaal Williams (aka JWilly) led the league with 17 rushing TDs thanks to the most carries (30) inside the 5... ever! His fantasy season was super TD-dependent but Dan Campbell seemed committed to the process. Williams will take his hip thrusting TD celly down to the Big Easy after he signed a 3 year deal with the Saints. While Alvin Kamara is one of the best backs in the league, New Orleans hasn't been afraid to limit his workload and given another back touches. Williams fits that role with potential upside for multiple RB1 weeks should Kamara miss time with his court date looming later this summer.

    J.D. McKissic

    Unsigned | 29 Years Old

    McKissic aka "Smooches" has been a thorn in the side of Antonio Gibson and Brian Robinson fantasy managers the last couple of seasons in Washington. He was cut by the team at the start of the league year after a neck injury ended his season prematurely last year. Expect McKissic to land a one or two-year deal as a pass catching specialist.

    signed

    Damien Harris

    Signed | 26 Years Old

    Bills, 1 year

    After a 2021 campaign where he finished with 15 rushing scores, Harris never was quite healthy all year long picking the least opportune time to get injured. He turns 26 years old and joins the Bills backfield. He'll compete for opportunities with 2nd year RB, James Cook. Devin Singletary (177 carries last year) is now with the Texans.

    signed

    Alexander Mattison

    Signed | 25 Years Old

    Vikings, 2 years

    Alexander Mattison has been waiting and biding his time in Minnesota as Dalvin Cook's backup. His time is now! Mattison is back in Minnesota on a 2-year deal and with Cook gone, he has a massive opportunity in front of him. He's still just 24 years old, and in six games without Cook on the field since Mattison entered the league, he's averaged 18.5 fantasy points per game.

    signed

    Jerick McKinnon

    Signed | 31 Years Old

    Chiefs, 1 Year

    It feels like this guy has lived nine dynasty lives as a Viking backup, a free agent signee of the 49ers, years of injuries, and now two years as the Chiefs late season RB godsend. McKinnon became the 2nd RB since 1995 with 9+ receiving TDs in a season joining Hall of Famer Marshall Faulk. He's getting up there in age but he's basically carved out a James White-esque role for the twilight years of his career. He's back in KC on a one-year deal.

    signed

    Chase Edmonds

    Signed | 27 Years Old

    Buccaneers, 1 year

    Remember Chase Edmonds? We certainly do and the Dolphins clearly regretted giving him a solid contract last off-season. They traded him to the Broncos, who cut him and saved $5.9 million with no repercussions. He lands in Tampa on a one-year deal. Who knows if he'll make the roster when the season starts.

    James Robinson

    Unsigned | 24 Years Old

    James Robinson was a revelation as an UDFA star for Jaguars before the team traded him to the Jets and he vanished into thin air. It remains to be seen what J Rob has left in the tank as the Patriots took a chance on him, signing him to a 2-year deal worth up to $8 million... until he was promptly cut three months later for mere beans.

    signed

    D'Onta Foreman

    Signed | 27 Years Old

    Bears, 1 year

    Foreman was one of the most volatile players in fantasy last year with weeks of 150+ rushing yards followed up by absolute duds. He's a bruiser and still capable of being a battering ram in a run heavy scheme. Well, what do you know? He lands in Chicago on a one-year deal. It remains to be seen if they keep adding to the position, but we expect Foreman to get touches alongside Khalil Herbert.

    signed

    Samaje Perine

    Signed | 27 Years Old

    Broncos, 2 years

    In the Bengals AFC Championship loss to the Chiefs, the veteran Perine out-snapped Joe Mixon by a wide margin (43 to 23) and looked like a solid chain-moving RB all year long. Dependability is the name of the game for Perine as far as his role for real life NFL purposes. Denver brought him in on a 2-year deal to add depth while Javonte Williams recovers from his multi-ligament knee injury. Look for Perine to carve out a role even when Williams is back from injury as he's considered one of the better pass protecting backs in the league.

    signed

    Rashaad Penny

    Signed | 27 Years Old

    Eagles, 1 year

    Penny secured a 1 year extension last year before inevitably succumbing to yet another injury. This poor guy's body hasn't been able to hold up in the NFL but when he's had the chance, he can be an absolute wrecking ball on the field averaging 5.6 yards per carry in his career. Now, he gets to play behind one of the league's best offensive lines in football in Philadelphia. Expect Penny to assume the Miles Sanders role, but other backs will likely get touches in this committee.

    Melvin Gordon III

    Unsigned | 30 Years Old

    Gordon re-signed with the Broncos last off-season before a clear falling out with the franchise leading to him being benched. His name might show up as part of the Kansas City Chiefs roster from 2022 but he failed to see one touch after being acquired in Week 12. Entering his age 30 season, Gordon is all but done for life in the NFL.

    signed

    Raheem Mostert

    Signed | 31 Years Old

    Dolphins, 2 Years

    We thought Colonel Mostert was on his last legs. However, Mike McDaniel is a devout believer willing to give him another chance. He signed a two year deal with up to $7.6 million in incentives. The Dolphins still will likely bring in another rookie to compete.

    signed

    D'Ernest Johnson

    Signed | 27 Years Old

    Jaguars, 1 year

    Johnson was on our radar last year as the Browns gave the former UDFA a second-round tender after he filled in admirably for the injured Nick Chubb and Kareem Hunt in 2021. He barely saw the field in 2022 with four total rush attempts. What were the Browns doing? Johnson lands in Jacksonville on a one-year deal. We'll see if he makes the team.

    signed

    Ronald Jones

    Signed | 25 Years Old

    Cowboys, 1 Year

    Rojo technically got a Super Bowl ring for his "work" in Kansas City last year. He saw just 17 carries in the regular season and one carry in the playoffs. He signed on with Cowboys as a veteran bruiser but without any guarantee he see much of the field. He is a two-time Super Bowl champion...

    signed

    Boston Scott

    Signed | 28 Years Old

    Eagles, 1 Year

    He's a fan favorite in Philly but he's nothing more than an annoying TD vulture at this stage in his career. At 5'6", he somehow stole goal-line looks away from Miles Sanders.

    signed

    Latavius Murray

    Signed | 33 Years Old

    Bills, 1 Year

    Did you have a 32-year-old Latavius Murray leading the Broncos in rushing yardage as part of your off-season analysis? The veteran RB just won't quit! He landed in Buffalo on a one-year deal and joins a RB depth chart that includes James Cook, Damien Harris and Nyheim Hines.

    signed

    Jeff Wilson Jr.

    Signed | 27 Years Old

    Dolphins. 2 years

    My Name is Jeff! He's back in South Beach on a two-year deal after the Dolphins decided to run it back with their RB duo from last year. He'll likely have useable weeks in fantasy given that this offense projects to be good once again, but it should be a week-to-week situation with Mostert stealing work.

    signed

    Sony Michel

    Signed | 28 Years Old

    Rams, 1 Year

    "I believe you've met my Fitness Consigliere... Michel." The veteran RB signed on again in Los Angeles to take up space on a roster behind Cam Akers to compete with Kyren Williams and Zach Evans for backup duties.

    David Johnson

    Unsigned | 31 Years Old

    Pour one out for the former fantasy star. He'll always have a special place in our hearts but it's also probably time for DJ to hang up those cleats.

    Kenyan Drake

    Unsigned | 29 Years Old

    Drake somehow led the Ravens in rushing TDs at the tender age of 28 on his last leg in the NFL. Gotta love the Drake!

    Joe Mixon

    2 Years Remaining with CIN | 26 Years Old

    He has two years left under contract but Cincinnati could save themselves nearly $10 million if they want to move on from the veteran RB. Samaje Perine actually out-snapped Mixon in the AFC Title game as it seemed like Mixon wasn't right physically to end the year. Perine is a free agent and seldom-used Chris Evans is the only other RB on the roster.

    Nyheim Hines

    2 Years Remaining with BUF | 26 Years Old

    Hines was curiously traded for at mid-season and then received 11(!) total touches from Week 9 on. He had two emotional kick return TDs a week after Damar Hamlin collapsed on the field, a moment that Bills fans will always remember. Despite Devin Singletary also being a free agent, the Bills can move on from Hines for $0 dead cap.

    • Free Agents
    • Cut Candidates

    DeAndre Hopkins

    Unsigned | 31 Years Old

    The Cardinals released the former All-Pro WR in late May after not being able to find a trade partner. Entering his age 31 season, D Hop is very likely to land on a Super Bowl contending roster. Teams like the Chiefs and Bills have been floating around in rumors, but for now it's wait and see mode on Nuk. Hopkins missed the first six weeks of the year last season, but when he returned to the lineup, he was still ultra productive and his underlying efficiency metrics were strong. Hopkins' days of being a fantasy football WR1 are probably over, but he can still absolutely provide WR2 numbers, especially if he's catching passes from Josh Allen or Patrick Mahomes. Stay tuned...

    signed

    JuJu Smith-Schuster

    Signed | 26 Years Old

    Patriots, 3 Years

    We always thought JuJu would be the prize of free agency... just not like this. With the WR market completely barren, Juju might be the best of what's remaining. He took a one year deal with Kansas City and was one of the few consistent options outside of Travis Kelce for the team almost hitting 1,000 yards on 101 targets. While his early career arc is a thing of the past, he landed a multi-year deal as a big slot receiver for the Patriots, a team in desperate need of a dependable possession weapon.

    signed

    Jakobi Meyers

    Signed | 26 Years Old

    Raiders, 3 Years

    Meyers went from unheralded UDFA to soaking up a team-leading 23 percent of the team's targets the last two years. He's not necessarily elite but is now reunited with former Patriots OC Josh McDaniels in Las Vegas. It's a nice payday for Meyers reaching a 3-year deal with $21 million guaranteed. As for fantasy, we likely have seen his best days but he can still return some FLEX value in PPR leagues for a few more years.

    signed

    Odell Beckham Jr.

    Signed | 30 Years Old

    Ravens, 1 year

    Odell Beckham missed all of 2022 recovering from a torn ACL, so we're not sure how much he has left in the tank at this point now that he's 30 years old. However, the Ravens paid him a lot of money on his one-year deal with the potential to earn up to $18 million in incentives, and this depth chart is wide open. That said, Beckham hasn't been fantasy relevant in years, so fantasy managers may have to re-shape what they think about this version of OBJ. He'll be 31 before the season is over.

    signed

    DJ Chark Jr.

    Signed | 26 Years Old

    Panthers, 1 year

    After signing a one year deal with Detroit last year, Chark might be one of the few down the field threats available on the market. He had three 90+ yard games down the stretch for a Lions team that almost made the playoffs. He's still pretty young (26) for someone five years into their NFL career and while the opportunity is there in Carolina, we're not typically too excited to invest in WRs catching passes from rookie QBs. Expect Chark to be a better "real life" WR for the Panthers than a fantasy threat.

    signed

    Allen Lazard

    Signed | 27 Years Old

    Jets, 4 Years

    After being tendered last off-season, Lazard entered 2022 as Aaron Rodgers' top pass-catching option after Davante Adams left town. He was solid in the first half of the year as the WR12 from Weeks 4-9 in fantasy before rookie Christian Watson took over as the top option. The former UDFA is a big-bodied red zone threat and cashed in on a 4-year deal with $22 million guaranteed in New York.

    signed

    Robert Woods

    Signed | 31 Years Old

    2 Years, Texans

    The Titans said siyanara to Woods saving them $12 million against the cap. His yards per route run plummeted (1.12) this past year as the Titans offense resembled something more like a Pop Warner team. The Texans added him shortly after his release to the tune of two years with $10 million guaranteed, which is not bad for a veteran entering his 11th season in the NFL. Woods should have some PPR appeal as a FLEX this season as a trusty possession WR for whoever they take at No. 2 overall.

    signed

    Darius Slayton

    Signed | 26 Years Old

    2 years, $12 million

    You might've forgotten but Slayton had a great rookie year (740 yards & 8 TDs) way back in 2019 before essentially being replaced on the depth chart by the likes of Kenny Golladay and Kadarius Toney. His dynasty life was resurrected in 2022 after the entire Giants WR room was injured or ineffective.
